Manufacturing Challenges

Project examples built around outcomes, not deliverables.

These customer-safe examples show the type of production problem, engineering approach, and practical outcome Eden Industrial Systems is built to support.

Reduce Setup Time

Machining fixture system

Flexible workholding concepts that improve part location, setup speed, and repeatability.

Problem Repeat jobs required manual locating, indicating, and clamp adjustment, making setup time difficult to predict.
Approach Develop a fixture concept with positive datums, repeatable mounting, accessible clamping, and chip-clearance considerations.
Outcome A more repeatable setup path with fewer operator-dependent decisions and clearer first-piece preparation.

Improve Manufacturability

DFM and component redesign

Review parts and assemblies for manufacturability, fabrication risk, and production efficiency.

Problem Part features increased machining difficulty, inspection effort, and vendor interpretation risk.
Approach Review geometry, datum strategy, tolerance stackups, tool access, and drawing clarity against real manufacturing constraints.
Outcome Cleaner manufacturing intent, reduced ambiguity for suppliers, and a design direction better suited for production.

Improve Assembly Consistency

Repeatable assembly support

Tooling and workstation concepts that reduce manual alignment and improve operator workflow.

Problem Assembly quality depended on operator feel, manual alignment, and repeated checking during the build.
Approach Create tooling concepts with nests, stops, alignment features, access for hands and tools, and a clearer assembly sequence.
Outcome A more controlled workflow that supports consistent loading, simpler training, and less rework from alignment variation.

Increase Capacity

Production aid design

Mechanical systems and production tools designed to remove friction from manufacturing operations.

Problem Material handling, staging, or manual movement slowed the operation and pulled attention away from value-added work.
Approach Design a practical aid around floor space, operator reach, part protection, maintenance access, and vendor-ready fabrication details.
Outcome A production-focused mechanical concept that reduces wasted motion and helps the team keep work moving predictably.
