Production Fixture Design

Turn Recurring CNC Jobs Into Faster, More Repeatable Setups

Eden Industrial Systems designs custom production fixtures and workholding for CNC shops that need to reduce setup time, simplify loading, improve repeatability, and recover machine capacity.

Request a Setup Review See the Design Process

Best Fit

Recurring work where setup time, operator variation, or one-at-a-time loading limits output.

  • Repeat production parts and part families.
  • Long or inconsistent setup times.
  • Prototype workholding that stayed in production.
  • Multi-part loading opportunities.
  • Internal engineering teams that need overflow support.

Who This Is For

For CNC job shops and OEM manufacturers running recurring work.

The strongest projects have a clear operational cost: too much setup time, too much operator dependence, too many re-clamps, or too little spindle time available for production.

  • High-mix, low- or medium-volume production.
  • Repeat jobs that return monthly, quarterly, or on standing orders.
  • Part families that could share a common fixture platform.
  • Jobs moving from prototype quantities into repeat production.
  • Bottleneck machines where setup time directly limits capacity.

Problems Solved

Common setup and workholding problems.

  • Long changeovers and first-piece approval.
  • Results that depend on one experienced machinist.
  • Excessive indicating, probing, and adjustment.
  • Too many re-clamps or manual handling steps.
  • Inconsistent clamping or poor datum control.
  • Temporary workholding still being used for production.

Fixture Types

Practical workholding designed around the job.

  • Multi-part VMC fixtures.
  • Fixture plates and pallet layouts.
  • Part-family and modular fixture systems.
  • Soft jaws, nests, stops, and locating systems.
  • Secondary-operation fixtures.
  • Tombstone and horizontal machining layouts.
  • Loading or inspection aids tied to CNC production.

Process

From current setup to build-ready fixture package.

  1. Review the current setup. Gather the part model or print, machine information, setup photos, quantities, and the specific bottleneck.
  2. Define success. Establish the target setup time, loading method, repeatability needs, operator requirements, and constraints.
  3. Develop the concept. Design the locating, clamping, access, chip-clearance, loading, and changeover strategy.
  4. Review before release. Confirm the concept with the customer and resolve manufacturability or process concerns.
  5. Release the package. Deliver the agreed CAD, drawings, BOM, hardware references, and setup notes.

Deliverables

Documentation that can be quoted, built, and used.

  • Fixture concept and layout.
  • 3D CAD assembly and part models.
  • Manufacturing and assembly drawings.
  • Bill of materials.
  • Clamp and purchased-hardware references.
  • Setup, loading, and review notes.
  • Vendor quote package when included in scope.

Customer Inputs

Information that makes the review useful.

  • Part drawing, model, or clear screenshots.
  • Current setup and workholding photos.
  • Machine make, model, travels, and table details.
  • Current setup and first-piece time.
  • Lot size and run frequency.
  • Current cycle time and parts per cycle, if known.
  • Known quality, chip, access, or operator issues.

Typical Scope

Focused mechanical design support.

  • Concept through detailed fixture design.
  • Design-only or design plus vendor coordination.
  • Revision support after customer or builder review.
  • Adaptation to existing vises, pallets, tombstones, and shop standards.
  • Overflow support for internal manufacturing engineering teams.

Initial Exclusions

Work that requires separate scope or another specialist.

  • Standalone CNC programming or process prove-out.
  • Large turnkey automation cells.
  • PLC or electrical controls as a standalone service.
  • Machine guarding certification or safety validation.
  • Guaranteed cycle-time or savings claims before machine validation.

FAQ

Common questions before a fixture project.

Do you manufacture the fixture?

Projects can be scoped as design-only, design plus vendor coordination, or design with build support. The exact responsibility is defined before work begins.

Can you work with our preferred fixture builder?

Yes. The design package can be prepared for your internal toolroom or an outside supplier, with review support included when needed.

Can you design around our existing workholding?

Yes. Existing vises, pallets, tombstones, zero-point systems, and standard hardware can be treated as project constraints.

Is every repeat job worth a dedicated fixture?

No. The setup review is intended to determine whether the run frequency, time savings, capacity value, and design complexity justify the project.

Who validates the fixture on the machine?

Machine prove-out and process validation are collaborative and must be defined in the project scope. Final production approval remains with the customer.
